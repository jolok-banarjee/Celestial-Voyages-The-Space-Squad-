Moons

Moons are natural satellites that orbit planets, dwarf planets, or other celestial bodies in our Solar System. They come in various sizes and compositions, from small, irregularly shaped rocky bodies to large, icy worlds. Moons play diverse roles, such as influencing tidal patterns, stabilizing planetary rotations, and, in some cases, potentially hosting conditions suitable for life.
