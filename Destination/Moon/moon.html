Overview

Moon tourism is now a reality. Experience the thrill of visiting another world, walking on the lunar surface, and witnessing Earthrise from the Sea of Tranquility.

About This Journey

The Moon is our closest celestial neighbor and the first destination for space tourism. From the historic Apollo landing sites to the stunning lunar highlands, this journey offers a unique blend of history, science, and adventure. Experience 1/6th gravity, walk where legends walked, and see Earth as a fragile blue marble suspended in the void.

Featured Destinations

Explore the most iconic and fascinating locations on the lunar surface.

The Selenean Summit

The highest point on the Moon at 10,786 meters, located on the far side. Nearly 20% taller than Mount Everest relative to its surroundings.

Rima Ariadaeus

A 300 km long linear rille formed by crustal faulting between Mare Vaporum and Mare Tranquillitatis. One of the most prominent rilles on the Moon.

Mare Tranquillitatis

The Sea of Tranquility — the first location on another celestial body visited by humans. Apollo 11 landed here in 1969, marking humanity's first steps on another world.

Mons Pico

A solitary lunar mountain in Mare Imbrium, 25 km long and rising 2.4 km high. Made of highly reflective anorthosite rock, it shines brilliantly against the dark mare.

Mons Hadley

A 4.6 km high massif in the Montes Apenninus range. The Apollo 15 mission explored its lower slopes, collecting some of the oldest rocks on the Moon.

Schroter's Valley

The largest sinuous rille on the Moon at 250 km long. Named after astronomer Johann Schröter, this valley reveals the Moon's dramatic volcanic past.

Lunar Grand Resort

Your home away from Earth during your lunar adventure.

About the Resort

Located in the Mare Tranquillitatis, the Lunar Grand Resort is built underground to protect guests from the harsh lunar environment — radiation, micrometeoroids, and extreme temperature swings. The resort features panoramic observation domes, Earth-view suites, lunar surface excursion vehicles, and a zero-G recreation center.


Resort Activities

  • Walk on the lunar surface with guided excursions
  • Explore lunar craters and mountains
  • Photography workshops in Earthrise viewing domes
  • Participate in ongoing scientific research
  • Stargazing without atmospheric interference

Travel Tips

Safety Tips

  • Be prepared for extreme temperature swings (-173°C to 127°C) and vacuum conditions
  • Always wear protective gear outside the resort — radiation levels are high without atmospheric shielding
  • Move carefully in 1/6th gravity — falls are slower but regolith is abrasive
  • Stay in contact with your guide at all times during surface excursions

Additional Tips

  • Get plenty of rest — lunar days are 29.5 Earth days long, adjust your sleep cycle accordingly
  • Stay hydrated — recycled water is precious, but dehydration is a real risk in pressurized environments
  • Exercise daily — low gravity causes muscle atrophy and bone density loss
  • Take care of your mental health — isolation and Earth-out-of-view phenomenon can be challenging
