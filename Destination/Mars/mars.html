Overview

Mars tourism is now a reality. Experience the thrill of visiting another world — from the red dunes of the surface to the ice caps at the poles and the Valles Marineris, one of the largest canyons in the solar system.

About This Journey

Mars is a beautiful and mysterious planet, and there's no place else like it in the solar system. You'll get to see the red dunes of the surface, the ice caps at the poles, and the Valles Marineris, one of the largest canyons in the solar system. You'll also get to meet the first humans to live on Mars — pioneers working to establish a permanent colony on the planet.

Travel Tips

Safety Tips

  • Be prepared for extreme cold (-63°C average) and thin atmosphere
  • Wear protective spacesuit, helmet, gloves, and boots at all times outside
  • Be careful where you step — rocks, dust, crevasses, and sinkholes
  • Stay in contact with your crew at all times
  • Be patient and respectful of the fragile Martian environment

Additional Tips

  • Get plenty of rest before and after your trip
  • Stay hydrated — Martian atmosphere is very dry
  • Exercise regularly to maintain muscle mass in zero-G
  • Take care of your mental health during the long journey
